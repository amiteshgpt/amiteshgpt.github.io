Professional Summary

  • PhD researcher studying the dynamic interactions between the terrestrial ecosystem and climate conditions.
  • Aim is to understand the widespread and complex patterns in ecosystem response to hydroclimatic dry extremes and elucidate their changing vulnerability.
  • Explores plant–soil–atmosphere processes leveraging eddy-covariance flux data and examines physiological and structural changes induced by extreme dryness in the land and atmosphere integrating satellite observations, reanalysis datasets, and climate-model simulations.
  • Motivated to simulate Earth's carbon and nitrogen cycles using an optimality-based dynamic global vegetation model to understand impacts of extreme perturbations in the energy and water cycles.

Projects

  • Burnt-area detection using SAR

    Developed a Sentinel-1 band-ratio-based classification workflow to detect burnt area amidst smoke, caused by wildfires in the Mediterranean region and tested over Canada.

  • Inundated-area mapping using SAR and Optical

    Developed an image-classification workflow integrating Sentinel-1 SAR and Sentinel-2 spectral data to identify inundated / damaged areas using stacked ML in Goa’s estuary.

  • Plant-resilience estimation using eddy-flux observations

    Developed a pipeline based on long-term ground observations (FLUXNET) to estimate the resilience of different ecosystems from disturbances caused by drought or heatwave across the USA.

  • Drought-recovery prediction using climate-model simulations

    Developed a workflow to predict the rate of drought recovery in different biomes and its changes from past to near-future using climate-model simulations.
